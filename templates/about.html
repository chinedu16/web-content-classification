About

The exponential growth of the Internet has led to a great deal of interest in developing useful and efficient tools and software to assist users in searching the web for relevant documents. Document classification is generally defined as a content-based assignment of one or more predefined categories to documents. Document classification appears in many applications, including email-filtering, news monitoring, etc. It is not feasible to classify these documents manually and present automated classification methods have drawbacks like low accuracy and dependency on humans for document tagging. The proposed system uses machine learning algorithms to speed up the classification process of the relevant documents. The proposed machine learning algorithm -’Multinomial Naive Bayes’ helps in the construction of a robust classifier model using a variety of data for training. This classifier can then be improvised to classify documents automatically. The Multinomial Naïve Bayes Classifier whose accuracy was approximately 99.81% was built and tested on data that was acquired from foreign and local news sources.

Taxonomies are used to classify content in an organized way to make it easy to navigate and find for other users, much the way a library catalogue works. However, recent research has shown that up to two-thirds of organizations cannot access their information assets talk less of their web articles or text documents due to poor information classification or tagging. Consequently, if everyone agrees that proper content classification helps you find the information you need, the question that businesses and other entities must ask themselves is how to classify the masses of content they face every day? There are many ways of doing this. In the past, this has often been a manual task, whereby users themselves placed content in different topics, folders, tags according to how they interpreted the content. The problem addressed in this project was to learn to classify (guess the category/group) unlabelled text documents and user input into eight specific classes of Economy, Education, Entertainment, Health, Law & Politics, Religion, Science and Technology & Sports. The problem would be solved by taking a large set of labelled (the category/group of the document) text documents and building a Naïve Bayes classifier from those documents which will be used to classify.

The main aim of this study is to be able to classify various input texts, text documents into eight specific classes of Economy, Education, Entertainment, Health, Law & Politics, Religion, Science and Technology & Sports. The objectives of this project include To explore the concept of text mining and classification. To comprehend the theoretical aspect of the Naïve Bayes classifier using the simple yet robust algorithm, Multinomial Naïve Bayes theorem to achieve the task of classification. To explore the concept of Natural Language Processing, study and implement the Naïve Bayes Algorithm. To train the Naïve Bayes algorithm with a set of training data consisting of columns of categories and sample articles. To discover how classification techniques can be used to classify unseen data into its various classes. To compute the performance of the classifier.
